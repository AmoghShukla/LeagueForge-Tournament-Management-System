Build a comprehensive League Tournament Management System inspired by the Indian Premier League that handles scheduling, operations, scoring, and analytics for a full season. The system must manage teams, venues, matches, and users with distinct roles while enforcing strict business rules. 
Each team must play every other team twice, and matches should be scheduled such that no team plays without a minimum gap of two days.
Venues have availability constraints, and matches must be dynamically scheduled without conflicts. 
The system should also support real-time score updates. Special scenarios like match rescheduling, cancellations, and tie-breakers must be handled. The focus is on building a robust, scalable system with strong validations, clear separation of responsibilities, and complex domain logic.

Expectations and functional requirements:

The system should manage teams, venues, and tournament seasons with proper validations and relationships.
A scheduler (admin) should generate fixtures ensuring round-robin (double matches), rest-gap constraints, and no venue conflicts.
Support dynamic rescheduling due to venue unavailability, weather conditions, or manual overrides.
Each match should move through states such as scheduled, live, completed, and cancelled with strict transition rules.
A venue manager should control venue availability, blackout dates, and capacity constraints.
A score updater should update ball-by-ball or aggregated scores and finalize results.
Viewers should access schedules, live scores, standings(points table or ranking), and historical data in read-only mode.
Track audit logs for all critical actions such as scheduling, updates, and cancellations.
Ensure proper validation, error handling, and consistency across concurrent operations.
